**The Problem:**
The app currently generates a random situation and tries to guess a verse. This leads to illogical answers (e.g., A scenario about "Phone Addiction" -> Answer: "Joseph's Treasury Verse"). This is confusing for the user.

**The Solution: Reverse-Engineered Scenarios**
You must change the generation workflow. Do not start with a topic. **Start with the Verse.**

**New Strict Workflow for Generating Exercises:**

1. **Step 1: Select the Target Verse FIRST.**
   - Example Verse: "فَصَبْرٌ جَمِيلٌ" (So patience is most fitting).

2. **Step 2: Extract the Key Concept.**
   - Concept: "Beautiful Patience" (Patience without complaint).

3. **Step 3: Write the Scenario to "Prime" the User.**
   - The Scenario MUST contain a paraphrase or the definition of the target words.
   - *Bad Scenario:* "Something bad happened. What do you say?" (Too vague).

* **Rule 2 (Contextual Fit):** Ensure the verse is used in its *linguistic* sense, not just theological.
* **Rule 3 (No Abstraction):** Do not use verses that require deep interpretation (Tafsir) to connect to the scenario. The link must be obvious.

**Task:**
Rewrite the prompt logic to ensure that every generated scenario includes **embedded clues** that lead directly to the specific words of the target verse.
